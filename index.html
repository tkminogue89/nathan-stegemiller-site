The work

Commissions

Every piece is made to order. We start from your space and the feeling you want in it, then I build a one-of-a-kind relief painting by hand in the studio — no two are alike.

  1. 01

    Inquiry & consultation

    Tell me about your space, dimensions, and the palette you're drawn to. We talk through scale, budget, and timeline.

  2. 02

    Concept & palette

    I share a direction — composition, colorway, and surface treatment — tailored to your room and refined together until it's right.

  3. 03

    Built by hand

    The piece is carved and layered in plaster and acrylic over several weeks, finished as a sculptural object made to catch raking light.

  4. 04

    Delivery & install

    Carefully packed and shipped, with local hand-delivery and installation available across the New York area.

Scale

From intimate single panels to large statement walls and multi-panel diptychs.

Materials

Hand-carved plaster, acrylic, and mixed media on cradled wood panel.

Timeline

Typically 4–8 weeks from approved concept, depending on size and queue.

Palette

Fully custom — muted greens, deep teals, cobalt, earthen neutrals, and beyond.

About

The artist

Nathan Stegemiller is a visual artist living and working in Brooklyn, New York. His relief paintings begin as carved fields of plaster, where winding negative-space channels are cut away and flooded with color — surfaces that sit somewhere between a topographic map and a coastline seen from above.

He works almost entirely on commission, building each piece by hand around the space it's destined for. Drawn to the meeting points of natural systems — where water finds its path, where ridgelines fold — he treats every wall as its own landscape.

His work lives in private homes and collections across the United States and Europe. He takes on a limited number of commissions at a time to keep each one personal.
