About

Nathan Stegemiller is a visual artist living and working in Brooklyn, New York. His relief paintings begin as carved fields of plaster, where winding negative-space channels are cut away and flooded with color — building surfaces that sit somewhere between a topographic map and a coastline seen from above.

Working primarily in muted greens, deep teals, cobalt, and earthen neutrals, Stegemiller is drawn to the meeting points of natural systems: where water finds its path, where ridgelines fold, where a single organic line organizes an entire plane. Each piece is built by hand, layer over layer, and finished as a sculptural object meant to catch raking light.

His work is held in private collections across the United States and Europe, and he accepts a limited number of commissions each year.
